# PM Frameworks

A working reference on how I actually run product. Not theory -- the frameworks I've built and refined over 15 years leading platform and infrastructure SaaS products.

Each file documents a specific framework: the mental model, a concrete example from my work, what broke, and what I'd do differently. These are practitioner notes, not prescriptions.

## Context

My background is platform and infrastructure SaaS -- observability, network monitoring, infrastructure automation, developer tooling. The frameworks here were built in that context. They're most directly applicable to:

- B2B SaaS with technical buyers
- Products with a self-serve or PLG motion alongside an enterprise sales channel
- Platforms where developer experience and API quality are competitive differentiators
- Companies going through a commercial model transition (usage-based, seat-based, consumption-based)

They may need adaptation elsewhere. I've tried to call out the assumptions that are context-specific.

---

## Why I built this

Most PM framework content is generic to the point of being useless. "Understand your users." "Align with stakeholders." "Measure what matters."

What's actually hard is the specific decision in a specific situation: how do you reprice a product when half your customer base is grandfathered into a model that no longer makes sense? What do you measure in week one of a trial when your product takes 30 minutes to set up? How do you know if churn is a product problem or a sales problem?

These files are my attempt to answer the specific version of the question, not the general one.
